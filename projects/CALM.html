Hand Tremor Mitigator

Developed a wearable device to mitigate hand tremors for individuals with motion disorders, combining IMU sensors and computer vision to stabilize utensil movement and improve precision in daily tasks.

Overview

Motion disorders and tremors significantly limit individual capabilities and quality of life, particularly in precision tasks. Traditional stabilization tools are expensive and uncommon. This project developed CALM (Cancellation of Arbitrary Limb Motion), a wearable tremor mitigation device using electronics for mass-producible solutions.

Methods

The project explored two primary approaches: Arduino with IMU sensors and Raspberry Pi with computer vision.

Arduino & IMU Approach: Used BNO055 IMU sensors to capture motion data and control servo-based gimbals for stabilization. Initial designs were desk-mounted but evolved to wrist-worn devices with improved ergonomics and 3-axis control.

Raspberry Pi & CV Approach: Implemented computer vision using OpenCV to differentiate tremors from intended movement. Used color masking to track utensils and calculate stabilization angles, with ESP32 for servo control.

Highlights

  • Developed multiple iterations of tremor mitigation devices, from desk-mounted to wrist-worn designs
  • Successfully integrated IMU sensors with servo gimbals for 3-axis stabilization
  • Implemented computer vision algorithms to distinguish tremors from intentional movement
  • Created lightweight, 3D-printed components for ergonomic wearable design
  • Explored sensor fusion techniques combining IMU and CV for improved accuracy

Results & Learnings

The project demonstrated the feasibility of electronic tremor mitigation but revealed challenges in differentiating tremors from movement and achieving smooth stabilization. Key learnings included HSV color tuning for CV, stabilization engineering principles, and sensor fusion techniques.

Future improvements could include triple-perspective angle calculations, smoother servos, and integration of IMU and CV methods for more robust detection and stabilization.
